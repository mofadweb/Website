About MoFAD

The Ministry of Fisheries and Aquaculture Development (MoFAD) is currently established by Executive Instrument (E.I) 28, in line with section 11 and 13 of the Civil Service Act, (PNDCL 327). It was first established by Executive Instrument (E.I) 1, 2013 on the 18th day of January 2013.

The Ministry is therefore required to initiate, cordinate and formulate policies and activities to promote the fishing industry as well as the emerging aquaculture sector in the country ins such a mannner as to reduce poverty amongst the stakeholders, create wealth and generally contribute to the national gross domestic product and foreign exchange earnings.

Mandate of the Ministry

The core mandate of MOFAD is to formulate and implement sector Policies and Strategies aimed at transforming the Fisheries and Aquaculture Sector to contribute more effectively to national development through science, technology, innovation and institutional capacity enforcement.

Vision

The Ministry of Fisheries and Aquaculture Development aspires to be a visible Ministry that promotes Accelerated Fisheries and Aquaculture Development to contribute to National Development.

Mission

The Ministry of Fisheries and Aquaculture Development exists to transform the Fisheries and Aquaculture Sector and industry into a viable economic segment and provide support services to promote private sector investments.

Values

To abide by democratic principles, rule of law, and to let knowledge have a liberative effect on all members and the Ghanaian society as a whole
