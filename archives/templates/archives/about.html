Ghana Union of Professional Students – GUPS is a democratic, nonpartisan, nonsectarian mass movement of Professional Students in Ghana. The Union’s outlook embraces all democratic, revolutionary, progressive ideals and principles, including those against imperialism, poverty, corruption, racism, tribalism, illiteracy for national development and socio-economic advancement.

The Ghana Union of Professional Students which began as a Society for Professional Institute (SPI) in the 1990’s had tertiary institutions like the Institute of Professional Studies now UPSA, the Ghana Institute of Journalism, The National Film and Television Institute (NAFTI), Ghana Institute of Languages (GIL), Ghana School of Surveying and Mapping (GSSM) and other professional institutes as it founding members.

The then Society for Professional Institute (SPI) evolved through constitutional and structural adjustment, to become the Ghana Union of Professional students in 2001 after more professional universities had been ratified into her fold. Currently, the union remains the single and largest umbrella body of professional students in the country. GUPS is now made up of 32 ratified institutions and is headquartered in the former PWD building adjacent the Independence Square, Accra.

Today, Ghana Union of Professional Students (GUPS) is made up of membership of over fifty-five Tertiary Institutions with an estimated students’ population of more than One hundred and fifty thousand students in Ghana who are matured students dominated.

Vision of the Union

To promote, improve, develop and safeguard the interests of professional students and Ghanaian students as a whole.

Realising

The paramount need to provide avenues for the training of dedicated, selfless and disciplined manpower imbued with the principles of probity, patriotism, accountability and integrity

Desirous

To participate actively in all national development programms of this country taking into consideration the importance of education in national development

Resolved

To abide by democratic principles, rule of law, and to let knowledge have a liberative effect on all members and the Ghanaian society as a whole

Recognizing

The full benefit of working together under a united front and within the framework of a common guiding spirit.
